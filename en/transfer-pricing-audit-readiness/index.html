Why proactive preparation makes sense

German and Swiss tax authorities have systematically expanded their transfer pricing audit capacity in recent years. Risk management systems identify companies with certain structural characteristics — without any specific suspicion being required.

A storyline developed under audit pressure comes too late. Documentation that cannot be produced within 30 days reverses the burden of proof. A substance question raised for the first time in the closing conference (Schlussbesprechung) cannot be remedied at that stage.

Audit readiness is not a state — it is a position that must be actively established and maintained.

Proactive vs. reactive

  • Proactive: Identify red flags, close documentation gaps, make the storyline consistent — on your own initiative, at a time of your choosing
  • Reactive: Defend under time pressure, close documentation gaps under audit conditions, coordinate the bilateral correction against a running assessment

The reactive situation is manageable. The proactive one costs less.

What audit readiness means

Documentation

Usable transfer pricing documentation under § 90 para. 3 AO must be producible within 30 days of the tax authority's request. It comprises the Master File, the Local File and — where applicable — the Country-by-Country Report. Documentation prepared only after the audit starts is treated as not submitted on time and reverses the burden of proof.

Storyline

Audit-ready transfer pricing has a coherent economic rationale — it explains why an intragroup transaction is structured the way it is. That rationale must be consistent: with the actual facts, with internal reporting, with external documentation. Inconsistencies between internal and external presentations are a classic audit trigger.

Substance

A Swiss entity that holds licences, provides services or acts as a holding company must demonstrate substantive economic activity in Switzerland. BEPS Action 5 and tightened ESTV practice have raised the bar. From a German perspective, the tax authority examines whether the substance in Switzerland is sufficient to justify the profit allocation claimed there.

Typical red flags

  • Intragroup service charges without a complete benefit test — the recipient cannot demonstrate economic benefit
  • Persistent losses at a group entity over multiple audit periods — a standard risk indicator in tax authority screening systems
  • Transfer pricing structure not consistently documented — differing descriptions in financial statements, TP documentation and internal reporting
  • No transaction matrix, or one that cannot be produced within 30 days — § 90 para. 3 AO obligation not met
  • Swiss entity with substance questions from a German perspective — profit allocation not supported by actual activity
  • Transfer pricing policy not updated following BEPS / OECD 2022 — particularly for services and IP structures
  • Inconsistencies between external documentation and internal management reporting — auditors compare both systematically
  • No bilaterally aligned position — DE and CH documentation are not coordinated, so an adjustment in one state automatically creates exposure in the other

Frequently asked questions

What is a TP quick check?

A structured initial assessment of your transfer pricing position in a short conversation. We identify which red flags exist in your structure — free, confidential, no engagement required.

What does the 30-day rule mean?

§ 90 para. 3 AO requires usable documentation to be submitted within 30 days of the tax authority's request. Documentation prepared only after the audit notice reverses the burden of proof.

What is the benefit test?

For intragroup services, the recipient must demonstrate a direct economic benefit from the service. Without this evidence, the tax authority may treat the charge as non-arm's length and disallow the deduction.

Does this cover Swiss entities?

Yes. The TP quick check covers the entire DE/CH structure — including substance questions under Swiss practice (ESTV) and reporting obligations. Both sides are assessed simultaneously.
