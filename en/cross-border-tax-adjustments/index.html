Focus area

Cross-Border Tax Adjustments

A profit adjustment in one state does not automatically produce relief in the other. Securing the corresponding correction in Germany or Switzerland requires active, coordinated steps — it does not happen by itself.

Get in touch Is my case a fit?
The core issue

When Germany makes a profit adjustment after a tax audit — whether on transfer pricing, hidden profit distributions or estimated income — Switzerland does not automatically reduce its tax base by the same amount. The corresponding correction must be formally claimed and substantiated on the Swiss side.

Typical situations

Upward adjustment in Germany, no Swiss relief

A German tax audit increases the taxable profit of a German entity based on its intercompany transactions with a Swiss group member. Without a corresponding downward adjustment in Switzerland, the same economic income is taxed in both countries — a clear case of double taxation that the DTA is meant to prevent.

Swiss correction pending

Where the primary adjustment has already been made in Germany and the taxpayer is waiting for Swiss relief, time limits and procedural requirements apply. Delay or inaction can forfeit the right to a corresponding correction entirely.

Hidden profit distributions

A non-arm's length benefit granted to a Swiss parent may be recharacterised as a hidden profit distribution in Germany, subject to German corporate tax and withholding tax. The Swiss parent may then need a corresponding adjustment to avoid double inclusion of the same income.

Estimated assessments with cross-border effects

Estimated assessments — often based on inferred profit allocations or assumed arm's length prices — regularly affect the Swiss tax position too. Without coordination, the estimate creates a double-tax burden that compounds over time.

A correction in one state creates an entitlement in the other — that must be actively enforced.

How we work

Assess the primary adjustment

We review the audit report and assess whether a corresponding correction in Switzerland is available and in what form. The analysis covers the Germany–Switzerland DTA, OECD guidelines and the domestic law of both states.

Claim the corresponding correction

We formally request the corresponding correction from the competent Swiss authority — the cantonal tax office or the SFTA — and substantiate the claim. The same process is managed in Germany if the adjustment flows the other way.

Challenge the primary adjustment in parallel

Where the primary adjustment is contestable, objection and appeal proceedings are pursued simultaneously. Both tracks — challenging the adjustment and securing relief — are managed under one coherent strategy.
