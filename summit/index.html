Five things you need to know about Swiss TP

01
No law, full exposure

Switzerland has no codified transfer pricing statute. The system runs on administrative practice and case law. The absence of a formal framework does not reduce exposure — it removes the formal protection that a statutory standard provides.

02
The audit that didn't come is not the audit that won't

The ESTV and Canton Zurich have been building dedicated TP units since 2023. Audit selection is increasingly data-driven — CbCR, BEPS compliance tools. Years without an audit are not years of compliance. They are years of accumulated exposure.

03
WHT on hidden distributions — Treaty Relief does not apply

If a price is assessed as non-arm's length, the difference is reclassified as a hidden distribution. If it was never booked as income, the withholding tax refund claim is structurally extinguished. Treaty relief applies to dividends — not to hidden distributions.

Ref: BVG A-416/2017, A-2677/2023

04
Cooperation duty exceeds documentation

In Switzerland, the taxpayer must construct the factual record. Failure to produce triggers a discretionary assessment. The absence of a formal prior documentation requirement is technically accurate — and entirely irrelevant in an audit.

05
IQR is necessary, not sufficient

The ESTV systematically targets routine entities with sub-3% margins, narrows benchmark sets to Swiss comparables, and applies strict annual periodicity. No multi-year averaging. A result within the interquartile range does not guarantee acceptance.

Don't let Switzerland surprise you.

Three things to do before the next audit

Concrete steps that follow from the five exposure points above.

Action 01
Check WHT exposure first

For every Swiss TP structure — is the withholding tax refund claim still intact if a correction is made? Check the bookings now, not in an audit. If a Verrechnungssteuer reclassification materialises and the income was never booked, the refund window may already be closed.

Action 02
Prepare for the production obligation

The cooperation duty exists regardless of prior documentation. Know what you will need to produce and how quickly before the audit request arrives. Discretionary assessment follows non-production — there is no cure after the fact.

Action 03
Establish a group policy on document transfer

Direct disclosure to foreign tax authorities without formal mutual legal assistance is illegal under Swiss law. This must be group-wide compliance policy before the next information request arrives — not a decision made under pressure in the moment.
